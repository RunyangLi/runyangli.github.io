Runyang Li

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in Mechanical Engineering at the Arizona State University, advised by Prof. Xiangjia Li. Previously, I am fortunate to work under the supervision of Prof. Huanbo Sun on tactile sensors ans tactile based dexterous manipulation and Prof. Yan Zhang on Multi scale Topology Optimization

In the era of Topology Optimization, Physical informed Machine Learning and Computer Graphics, my research goal is to constrcut foundation model that coupled geometry, physics and material properties. To achive the goal, my former research interests mainly focus on:

  • Topology Optimization: Exploring principled initialization strategies for improving the robustness and solution quality of non-convex optimization (Topology Optimizatio problem).
  • Inverse design of microstructures: Employ both shape and topology optimization to obtain microstructure with enhanced mechanical property and also involve the strategy into multi scale problem
  • Physical informed Machine Learning: Envolve machine learning system with physical intuitivity and geometry awareness for complex physical processes including heat conduction/diffusion and large scale and non-linear deformation
  • Physical interatction with real world: Construct tactile system with 3D contact awareness for sim2real and functional mechanisms (i.e Bistable strutcure)

If you would like to discuss my research or potential collaborations further, feel free to contact me. I'm open to connect and collaborate with each other.
